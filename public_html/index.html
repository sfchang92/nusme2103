My background

K S Lee obtained his BSc(Hons) from the University of Manchester in 1977, MSc and PhD from UMIST in 1978 and 1982 respectively. He joined the Department of Mechanical Engineering, National University of Singapore in March 1982 as a Lecturer. He was promoted to Senior Lecturer in 1988 and Associate Professor in 1998 respectively. In 1989, he received the Outstanding Young Manufacturing Engineer Award from Mr Frank J Riley Jr, President of the Society of Manufacturing Engineers (USA). He is currently heading the intelligent plastic injection mould design research & development program in the department.

Research interests

  1. Intelligent mould design techniques
  2. Rapid tooling/rapid mould making process in plastic injection mould
  3. Design and Manufacturing automation
  4. In-process tool wear monitoring in machining
  5. Flow forming of polymer pipes
  6. High speed machining

Research projects

  1. Development of an intelligent plastic injection mould design and assembly (IMOLD) system for the injection mould design industry
  2. Hot runner design in plastic injection mould
  3. Computer aided process planning in plastic injection mould
  4. On line tool condition monitoring using a PC
  5. Microwave application in rubber curing
  6. Flowforming of polymer pipes
