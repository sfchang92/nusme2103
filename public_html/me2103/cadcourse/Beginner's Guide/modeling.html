The following guides will help you begin using Solidworks to model parts, assemble components, and to create drawings.

Modeling:

1.1) To start modeling, open up solidworks software:
(1) To create a new part, select New as indicated in the red box.
(2) To open up an existing part file, select Open as indicated in the blue box.

OPTIONAL:
Before you create a part document, you may want to change the colour highlight to green to be consistent with ME2103 notes and tutorials elearning created by the lecturer. The default is blue.
Select Options as indicated in the green box. Select Colors from the left side bar and under Current color scheme, select Green Highlight and click OK.





When clicked on New, if you are using Solidworks without any given template, a box will appear as shown below. Select Part and click OK to create a new Part file.



If you are using a computer at the PC clusters in school, ME2103 template is available. Click on Advanced on the bottom left corner and the ME2103 template will appear as shown below. To create a new part file, select ME2103_Part as indicated in the red box, and click OK.




1.2) Before you begin, you may wish to customize your window so that the most frequently used tools are easily accessed.

Right click on the top of your window. You should see a toolbar menu. Ensure that the Sketch toolbar, the Features toolbar and the Standard Views toolbar are selected. You can drag the toolbars and dock them at the side, below current toolbars or even at the bottom of the window.



1.3) To start modeling, you have to first select a plane you want to sketch on. The planes are as shown in the red box below.
(Note: If you select the Front or Top or Right plane, you will be sketching the Front view, Top view, Right view of the part respectively.)



1.4) After selecting a plane, you can start sketching by selecting the Sketch Tab as shown, and selecting the shapes you want to sketch. (The most commonly used will be rectangle and circle.)



1.5) After selecting the desired shape, click on the origin (as shown in the red box). Move the cursor to adjust the size of the rectangle/circle and left-click to complete the sketch.



1.6) To change the dimension of a rectangle/circle, click on Smart Dimension as shown.



Select the desired length/breadth/circumference to be changed and the dimension will appear. Move the cursor and left-click to place the dimension.
A pop-out box will appear as shown below. You can type the desired dimension and Enter, or simply click on the tick on the pop-out box.
(If dimension is in mm, you can omit adding the unit behind the value.
Which means, for example, if you want the dimension to be 50mm, you just have to type 50. However, it only applies for mm, you cannot omit the unit if you're typing a value in cm.)



For example, to change the distance between the center of a circle to the length of the rectangle as shown, simply click on Smart Dimension and select the center of the circle and length of the rectangle.



1.7) To change the dimension again, double click on the dimension and the pop-out box will appear again.

1.8) To view the different views (Front/Back/Left/Right/Top/Bottom/Isometric) of the part, you can select the desired view from the Standard Views Toolbar as shown in the red box.
(Standard Views Toolbar can be created by right-clicking on the top of the window and selecting Standard ViewsStandard Views.)
Alternatively, you can click on the View Orientation as shown in the blue box and select the desired view.



To zoom in, zoom out or rotate the view: Right-click and move cursor to Zoom/Pan/Rotate as shown and select the desired action. Press Esc after you are done.
Alternatively, you can zoom in and out by rotating the scroll wheel on your mouse. To rotate view, click and hold on to the scroll wheel and move your mouse.
