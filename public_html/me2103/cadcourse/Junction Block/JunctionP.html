Step 1: Creating the Body

1.1) To create a new document: Click on New and select   to create a new Part document.

1.2) To create a rectangular solid: Select Right plane in the Features Manager and click on Sketch  .  

a. Select Rectangle and sketch a rectangle of any size. Click on Smart Dimension , change the length of the rectangle to 74mm ( 12 + 25 + 25 +12 ) and the breadth to 109mm ( 76 + 48 + 23 - 38 ).

b. Click on Extruded Boss/Base , select type Blind and set depth at 64 mm. Click on   to finish.

Step 2: Creating the Base

2.1) To create the smaller base: Click on bottom view and select the bottom face as the sketching plane.

a. Click on Rectangle and sketch a rectangle with its left edge coincident with the left edge of the plane. Next, click on Smart Dimension and set the length and breadth of the rectangle to 52 mm. Position the sketch as shown.

     

b. Click on Extruded Boss/Base , select type Blind and set depth at 23 mm ( 38 -15 ). Click on   to finish.

 

 

 

 

 

 

2.2) To create the larger base: Select the bottom face of the smaller base created in Step 2.1 as the sketching plane.

a. Click on Rectangle and sketch a rectangle. Next, click on Smart Dimension to set the length and breadth of the rectangle to 126 mm. Position the rectangle as shown.

         

b. Click on Extruded Boss/Base , select type Blind and set depth at 15 mm. Click on to finish.

a. Click on Circle and sketch a circle. Next, click on Smart Dimension and set the diameter to 12 mm. Position the circle as shown.

    

b. Click on Extruded Cut , select type Blind and set depth at 15 mm. Click on to finish.

c. Under 'Direction 2', select the edge indicated as Direction 2 in the diagram below. Click on Reverse Direction if necessary to ensure the copies are in the correct orientation. Set Number of Instances to 2 and the Spacing as 100 mm. Click on to finish.

Step 3: Creating the Circular Boss

 

3.1) To create the circular boss: Select the side of the solid model highlighted in green as shown below as the sketching plane.


a. Click on Circle and sketch a circle. Click on Smart Dimension and set its diameter to 44 mm. Position the center of the circle at 76 mm above the bottom of the large square base and 25 mm away from the right side of the sketching plane as shown.


b. Click on Extruded Boss/Base , select type Blind and set depth at 39 mm ( 76 -12 - 25 ). Click on to finish.

 

 

 

 

 

 


 

3.2) To create the hole on the circular boss: Select the circular face of the circular boss created as the sketching plane.

a. Click on Circle and sketch a circle sharing the same center as the circular face selected. Click on Smart Dimension and set its diameter to 20 mm.

b. Click on Extruded Cut , select type Blind and set depth at 51 mm ( 39 + 12 ). Click on   to finish.

 

 

     

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 



Step 4: Creating the Square Boss

 

4.1) Defining the Sketching plane with aid of reference geometry: To create the sketching plane, click on Reference Geometry -> Plane. Select the circular face highlighted in the diagram as 'First Reference'. Change the Distance to 152 mm and make sure the inserted plane is on the opposite side as the circular boss. (Check the Flip box if necessary ). Click on   to finish.

 

 

     

 

 

 

4.2) To create the square boss: Select the plane created in Step 4.1 as the sketching plane.

a. Select Back View. Click on Rectangle and sketch a rectangle with its top edge coincident with the top edge of the model. Click on Smart Dimension and set its length and breadth to 46 mm. Position the sketch as shown in the diagram below.


b. Click on Extruded Boss/Base , select type Up to Surface and select the curved surface highlighted in pink in the diagram below. Click on to finish.

     

 

 

 

 

 


4.3) To create the hole on the square boss: Select the square face of the boss created in Step 4.2 as the sketching plane.

a. Click on Circle and sketch a circle. Click on Smart Dimension and set its diameter to 20 mm. Position the circle at the center of the square face.

 

 

 

 

 

 

b. Click on Extruded Cut , select type Blind and set depth at 76 mm. Click on   to finish.

 

 

 

 

 

 



Step 5: Creating the Loft

5.1) Sketching the first circle that defines the loft: Click on Left and select the face as shown below as the sketching plane. Click on Circle and sketch a circle. Click on Smart Dimension and set its diameter to 58mm. 

Position its center 90mm above the base of the entire model and 37 mm ( 25 + 12 ) away from either sides of the body created in Step 1. Click on to exit sketch.


5.2) Sketching the second circle that defines the loft:

a. First, create the sketching plane. Click on  Reference Geometry -> Plane. Under 'First Reference', select the back face of the solid model as shown. Change the Distance to 31 mm and ensure that the inserted plane is away from the model. (Check the Flip box if necessary ).Click on to finish.

   

b. Select the plane created in Step 5.2a as the sketching plane. Click on Circle  and sketch a circle concentric with the 58mm circle sketched in Step 5.1. Click on Smart Dimension and set its diameter to 44 mm. Click on to exit sketch.

 

5.3) To create the loft:

a. Click on 'Lofted Boss/Base' . Select the 58 mm circle ( from Step 5.1) and the 44 mm circle (from Step 5.2) as the 'Profiles'. (Be careful when u select the circles otherwise the loft will be distorted!). Click on to finish.

         

 

5.4) To create the hole on the loft: Select the circular face of the loft as the sketching plane.

a. Click on Circle and sketch a circle sharing the same center as the circular face of the loft. Click on Smart Dimension and set its diameter to 25 mm. 

b. Click on Extruded Cut , select type Blind and set depth at 43 mm ( 31 + 12 ). Click on   to finish.

 

 

 


Step 6: Creating the Ribs

6.1)To create the slanted rib:

a. First, create the sketching plane. Click Reference Geometry -> Plane. Select the circular face highlighted in the diagram as 'First Reference'. Change the Distance to 76 mm and make sure the inserted plane lies in the middle of the solid model. (Check the Flip box if necessary). Click on to finish.

     

b. Select the plane created in Step 6.1a as the sketching plane. Select Front View. Click on Line and sketch a line connecting the bottom edge of the body and the upper edge of the square base as shown beloew.

     

c. Click Rib . Select Both Sides and change Distance to 12 mm. If necessary, select Flip material side to ensure direction of rib is inwards. Click on to finish.

     

  

 

 

 

6.2) To create the second rib:

a. First, create the sketching plane. Click Reference Geometry -> Plane. Select the rectangular face highlighted in the diagram as 'First Reference'. Change the Distance to 69 mm and make sure the inserted plane lies in the middle of the solid model. (Check the Flip box if necessary). Click on  to finish.

   

b. Select the plane created in Step 6.2a as the sketching plane. Select Right View. Click on Line and sketch a vertical line connecting the top edge of the base and the mid section of the curved surface.



c. Click Rib . Select First Side and change Distance to 12 mm. If necessary, select Flip material side to ensure direction of rib is inwards. Click on to finish.

     

   

 

6.3) To create the third rib:

a. First, create the sketching plane. Click Reference Geometry -> Plane. Select the face highlighted in the diagram as 'First Reference'. Change the Distance to 57 mm and make sure the inserted plane lies in the middle of the solid model. (Check the Flip box if necessary). Click on to finish.

       

b. Select the plane created in Step 6.3a as the sketching plane. Select Front View. Click on Line and sketch a vertical line connecting the top of the base and the mid section of the curved surface at 13mm from the edge of the base. Click on Smart Dimension and set the distance to be 13mm.


c. Click Rib . Select Second Side and change Distance to 12 mm. If necessary, select Flip material side to ensure direction of rib is inwards. Click on to finish.

      

   

 

6.4) To create the fourth rib:

a. First, create the sketching plane. Click Reference Geometry -> Plane. Select the rectangular face highlighted in the diagram as 'First Reference'. Change the Distance to 63 mm and make sure the inserted plane lies in the middle of the solid model. (Check the Flip box if necessary). Click on to finish.

   

b. Select the plane created in Step 6.4a as the sketching plane. Select Left View. Click on Line and sketch a vertical line connecting the edge of the base and the bottom of the aquare boss.


c. Click Rib . Select Both Sides and change Distance to 12 mm. If necessary, select Flip material side to ensure direction of rib is inwards. Click on to finish.

       

   

Step 7: Redefining the Model


7.1) Refining the Junction Block: Click on Fillet . Set the fillet radius to 3mm and apply fillet to the edges as shown below. Click on to finish.

 

7.2) To save the part: You can now save this file by clicking File -> Save. Save this solid part as "Junction Block".

Drawings

Required:

1. Add 2 sectional views as indicated by the cutting planes X-X and Y-Y.

2. Add a plan view as indicated by the arrow E.

3. Add 5 dimensions indicated with *.

4. Indicate clearly the angle of projection that you are using.

8.1) To create a new drawing document : Click on New    and select     to create a new drawing.

8.2) To import the Junction Block file : Under 'Part/Assembly to Insert', click on Browse and select the Junction Block part saved in Step 7.3.

8.3) To add a Model view : Under 'Orientation', select Right   . Under 'Display Style', select Hidden lines removed. Under  'Scale', select Use Custom Scale and set scale as 1 : 2. Position the first view as shown. Click on or Press Escape on keyboard to finish.


8.4) To add a Projected view : Click on Projected view , position the second view as shown and click on the drawing sheet to place the view. Click on or press Esc on keyboard to finish.

  

 

 

 

8.5) To create section view X-X: Click on the second view to set it as the active view. Click on Section View . Ensure that the cutting line is vertical. Position the cutting plane such that it passes through the center as shown and click to place it on the view. 

 Click on  and a Section Scope window will appear. Click on the  beside Drawing View 2. Select all the Ribs from the drop down list to exclude hatching. Ensure that the cutting is in the correct direction. 

Click Ok. Place the sectional view as shown.

Change the alphabet to X and click on.

8.6) To create section view Y-Y : Click on the first view to set it as the active view. Click on Section View . Ensure that the cutting line is vertical. Place the cutting plane such that it cuts through the center as shown.

Click on and a Section Scope window will appear. Select all the Ribs to exclude hatching. Check Flip direction and ensure that the cutting is in the correct direction. Click Ok and place the sectional view as shown.

Change the alphabet to Y and click on.

8.7)  Changing the line style : 

Select Options:

Click on Document Properties -> View Labels -> Section.
Under 'Line Style', select the last line style from the drop down list. Click Ok.



8.8) To create the Plan View : 

Select the first view and click on Projected View . Place the plan view as shown and click ,

Right click the sectional view X-X, go to Alignment and click on Break Alignment. Do the same for sectional view Y-Y and the plan view. Reaarange the views as shown.

Next, add an Isometric View. Click on Model View and repeat the procedures in step 8.2 and 8.3 except under 'Orientation', select Isometric. Place the Isometric View as shown. Click .

8.10) Editing tangent edges : Right click on the Plan view, go to Tangent Edge and select Tangent Edges with Font. Do the same for the Isometric view. As for the remaining sectional views, select Tangent Edges Removed.

 

8.11) Adding center marks and centerlines : Click on Center Mark. Select the 4 holes on the plan view and the holes on the sectional views respectively. Click .

Select sectional view Y-Y and sketch the centerlines accordingly as shown.

Sketch the centerlines for the remaining 2 views.
