Step 1: Creating the Base

1.1) Click on New and select   to create a new part document

1.2) Select the Top plane (you have to select a plane before you can sketch). Click on Sketch . 

 

CREATING THE MAIN BODY

1.3) Using the Line , Circle , 3-point Arc , Trim , Smart Dimemsion and Add Relation tool, sketch the profile as shown with its dimensions.

1.4) Click on Extruded Boss/Base and type Depth 20 mm. Click on  . As shown.

1.5) On top of new solid surface, Using the Circle  and Smart Dimemsion tool, sketch the profile as shown with its dimensions.

CREATING THE SPECIFIC FEATURES ON THE MAIN BODY

Step 6: Assembly

6.1) Click on New and select  to create a new assembly.

6.2) Under "Part/Assembly to Insert", click on Browse and select Base.SLDPRT. Click anywhere on the right window to place the part. This action will set the part "Base" as the base for the assembly, i.e. it is fixed and cannot be moved.
6.3) To impart the Bottom into the assembly: Click on Insert Component , Browse and locate Bottom.SLDPRT. Click in the right window to place the jaw grip.

6.4) a. Click on Mate . Select the flap surface of the Base and Bottom as shown. Select Coincident . Click on    when done.

(Note: Click on Rotate View , Zoom In/Out , Zoom to Area and/or Pan to rotate, zoom in/out and/or move the model to aid in the selection of the faces.)

b. Select the cylinder surface of the Base and Bottom as shown. Select Concentric . Click on    when done.
              


6.5) a. Click on Mate . Select the flap surface of the Bottom and Rubber Foot as shown. Select Coincident . Click on    when done.

b. Select the cylinder surface of the Bottom and Rubber Foot as shown. Select Concentric . Click on    when done.

c. Repeart action 6.5.b for another three Rubber Foot.

6.6) a. Click on Mate . Select the flap surface of the Base and Collar as shown. Select Coincident . Click on    when done.

b. Select the cylinder surface of the Base and Collar as shown. Select Concentric . Click on    when done.



6.7) a. Click on Mate . Select the flap surface of the Jug and Collar as shown. Select Coincident . Click on    when done.

b. Select the cylinder surface of the Jug and Collar as shown. Select Concentric . Click on    when done.

c. Select the front plane of the main assembly body and  the front plan of sub-assembly body "Jug" as shown. Select Angle with 45 degree. Click on    when done.


6.8) a. Fristly hide the Jug, and then Click on Mate . Select the flap surface of the Base and Blade Housing as shown. Select Coincident . Click on    when done.

b. Select the cylinder surface of the Collar and Blade Housing as shown. Select Concentric . Click on    when done.

 

6.9) a. Click on Mate . Select the flap surface of the Lower Blade and Blade Housing as shown. Select Coincident . Click on    when done.

b. Select the cylinder surface of the Lower Blade and Blade Housing as shown. Select Concentric . Click on    when done.

6.10) a. Click on Mate . Select the flap surface of the Lower Blade and Upper Blade as shown. Select Coincident . Click on    when done.

b. Select the edges of the Lower Blade and Upper Blade as shown. Select Coincident . Click on    when done.



6.11) a. Unhide the Jug, Click on Mate . Select the flap surface of the Jug and Rubber Lid as shown. Select Coincident . Click on    when done.

b. Select the cylinder surface of the Jug and Rubber Lid as shown. Select Concentric . Click on    when done.

6.12) a. Click on Mate . Select the cylinder surface of the Rubber Lid and Cap as shown. Select Concentric . Click on    when done.

b. Select the flap surface of the Rubber Lid and Cap as shown. Select Coincident . Click on    when done.

6.13) You can now save this file by clicking File -> Save. Save this assembly as "Blender".

Drawings

Required:

a) a fully assembled sectional front elevation on A-A corresponding to the front elevation of the base.

b) a plan view of the assembly

c) an end elevation looking in the direction of arrow B

d) an isometric view

CREATING THE PLAN VIEW

Before you begin, right click on the top of the window and check the Sketch toolbar and the Annotations toolbar. Click and Drag them to the side of the window. This allows us to access the tools easily.

7.1) Click on New and select  to open a new drawing file from the ME2103 template. Click Browse, and open Blender.SLDASM.

7.2) Under "Orientation", select Front . Set the "Custom scale" to 1:5. This is the scale which we are going to use for the whole drawing. Left click on the desired location of the drawing sheet to place the view. Click  to finish.

   

To set the Tangent Edge style, right-click on the view. From the drop-down menu, go to Tangent Edge and select Tangent Edges Removed.

 

ADDING THE SECTION VIEW A-A

7.3) Click on Section View . Select Vertical cutting line  and left click to place the cutting plane such that it passes through the center.

Click on  and the section view box will appear. Make sure you check the auto hatching box. Click ok.

 

**Changing the line style: 

Select Options:

Click on Document Properties -> View Labels -> Section.
Under 'Line Style', select the last line style from the drop down list. Click Ok.

ADDING THE END ELEVATION VIEW

7.4) Click on Projected View . When prompt to select, click on the drawing of Section View A-A. Move the pointer to the left side (to get an end elevation view from direction B and left-click to place the view. Click to finish.). To set the Tangent Edge style, right-click on the section view and end elevation view to set Tangent Edges Removed.

 
ADDING THE ISOMETRIC VIEW

7.5) Click on Model View . Double-click on the Blender document. Under "Orientation", select Isometric and set "Custom Scale" to 1:5. Place the isometric view as shown. To set the Tangent Edge style, right-click on the isometric view. From the drop-down menu, go to Tangent Edge and select Tangent Edges Removed.

  

ADDING ANNOTATIONS

7.6) To create the centerlines using Centerline   from the Annotations Toolbar (not this ):

Before creating the centerlines, click on the plan view and under Display Style, select Hidden Lines Visible. Do the same for the end elevation view.

Click on Centerline and under Auto Insert, check Select View.

Select the plan view and isometric view. Click on  to finish.

After creating the centerlines, click on the isometric view and under Display Style, select back Shaded with Edges.

Centerlines created on the three views:
*Right click and hide unnecessary hidden lines created.


7.7) Select Center Mark  from the Annotations Toolbar. Select the respective features on the drawings to add in the center marks as shown.

ADDING DIMENSIONS

7.8) Use Smart Dimension  to add the 5 dimensions indicated with an *. (Deselect Add Parenthesis  to remove the parenthesis.)

LABEL THE VIEWS AND FILL IN TABLE

7.9) Click on Note   to add/edit the labels of the respective views. Fill in your Name, Matric number, Class, Scale, Date, Tutorial Name and Comments. 

 


LABEL SYMBOL OF PROJECTION ANGLE

7.10) Click on Note   and Add Symbol. Selection the correct projection symbol from symbol library.

 
 

7.11) You can now save this file by clicking File -> Save.
