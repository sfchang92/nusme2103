Step 1: Sketching Circles

1.1) Click on New and select    to create a new part document.

1.2) Select Front Plane. (You have to select a plane before you can sketch.)

1.3) Click on Circle and sketch a circle centered at the origin.

1.4) Click on Smart Dimension and set its diameter to 44 mm.

1.5) Sketch another concentric Circle of diameter 20 mm as shown.

          
NEW - Use of Centerline to locate desired positions
1.6) Click on Centerline and sketch a vertical Centerline from the origin as shown. Press Esc to deactivate the Centerline tool.

1.7) Sketch another Centerline and add two dimensions: Length 50 mm, 15º away from the 1st centerline.

 

1.8) Using the end point of the second centerline as the center,
a. Sketch a Circle of diameter 30 mm.
b. Sketch a smaller concentric Circle of diameter 12 mm.

   

Step 2: Tangent Line

2.1) Sketch a Line on the left of the circles as shown. Press Esc to deactivate the Line tool.

2.2) Hold Ctrl and select the line and the top outer circle with which the line is to be tangent to. Ensure that only the line and the circle have been selected. Under "Add Relations", select Tangent  and click on to finish. (For every successful application of the tangent relation, the symbol  appears.)

NB: 'Ctrl' allows the simultaneous selection of multiple objects.

               

2.3) Hold Ctrl and select the line and the bottom outer circle. Select Tangent again. Click on to finish.

2.4) Trim the line by using Trim to obtain the following as shown. (Note:- Under Options in the Trim Properties Manager, select ‘Trim to closest’    ).

   


Step 3: Mirror Entities

 3.1) Sketch a horizontal Centerline  starting from the origin to the right with length of 60 mm. From its end point, sketch another vertical Centerline  upwards with height of 6 mm.

     

3.2) From the end point of the 2nd centerline obtained from Step 3.1,

a. Sketch a horizontal Line  to the left with length 25 mm.

b. Sketch a vertical Line  upwards with height 12 mm.


New - Use of Mirror Entities to produce a mirror image of selected lines about an axis.

3.3) Hold Ctrl and select the 2 lines sketched in Step 3.2.

   

3.4) Click on Mirror Entities . Click on the box under "Mirror about" and select the 60mm centerline created. Click on  to finish.

   

Step 4: Tangent Arc and Tangent Line

 4.1) Click on Tangent Arc and draw an arc connecting the ends of the 25 mm lines as shown.

   

4.2) Sketch a Line  as shown. 

   

Using the method discussed in Step 2, tangent the line to the bottom outer circle. Trim  the excess part of the line. (Note:- Under Options in the Trim Properties Manager, select ‘Trim to closest’   ).

      

Step 5: Locating the Center of an Arc

5.1) Required: 
An arc of radius 70 mm which is in contact with point B and the 30mm circle centered at A. 

Technique:
a. Centered at A, sketch a Circle of radius (15+70) = 85 mm. This will ensure that the resultant arc of radius 70 mm will meet the 30mm circle at one point only.

b. Centered at B, sketch a Circle of radius 70 mm to ensure that the resultant arc will pass through point B.

c. There will be two intersection points between these two circles. The intersection point X is the correct point which will produce the desired arc.


　

5.2) Centered at the intersection point X, sketch a Circle of radius 70 mm.



5.3) Hold Ctrl button and select the two circles sketched in Step 5.1. Press Delete button to remove them. A dialogue box entitled 'Sketcher Confirm Delete' will appear. Click Yes to All.


5.4) The result of Step 5.3 is a circle of radius 70 mm which passes through point B and a point on the 30mm circle centered at point A.

Step 6: Trimming

The aim of this step is to remove certain curves so as to obtain the desired base sketch which can then be extruded to form the base model.

6.1) Select and delete all 3 centerlines sketched in the previous steps. (Failure to delete the centerlines may lead to error messages during base extrude in Step 7.) Most parts of the sketch will turn blue in color indicating that they are under-defined because they were referenced from or as a result of the centerlines.


6.2) Click on Trim . Under Options in the Trim Properties Manager, select ‘Trim to closest’  . Next, click on the segments circled in red in the diagrams below to obtain the desired sketch.

8.1) Select the plane shown below in green. Click on Sketch .


8.2) Click on Hidden Lines Visible . Sketch a Rectangle as shown in the diagram below with breath 14 mm, length 40 mm, 8 mm away from both the front and back face.

  
8.3) Click on Extruded Cut , select Through All, check 'Direction 2', and change to Through All. Click on  to finish and select back Shaded with Edges.




8.4) Select the front face as shown.

a. Sketch a Circle of 40 mm as shown and a Rectangle of breath 9 mm, starting from the center of the circle sketched. The length of the rectangle is not important but it must extend outside the model.

NEW - Creation of a Reference Plane and the use of it with Mirror Feature to duplicate features about a reference plane.

8.5) Click on Reference Geometry and select Plane .

Drawings

Required:
a. 3 orthographic views (Hidden details are not required)

    (1) A Front Elevation indicated by the arrow A.

    (2) A Plan View.

    (3) A Side Elevation indicated by the arrow B.

b. Indicate clearly the angle of projection.

CREATING THE FRONT, END & PLAN VIEW
9.1) Click on New and select  to create a new drawing document.

Click on Standard 3 View . Click 'Browse' and go to the location of your "Toggle Lever" part file.

9.2) To set the Tangent Edge style, right-click on the front view, from the drop-down menu, go to ‘Tangent Edge’ and select ‘Tangent Edges Removed’. Do the same for the other 2 views.

9.3) The generated views use the default scale of the sheet i.e. 1:2. To change the scale, so that the drawing views occupy the sheet more fully, click on the Front View. The Drawing View manager appears. In the Scale box, click on 'Use custom scale', and select 1:1.

NB: The other views (End Elevation and Plan View) will automatically scale up to 1:1 because, by default, they follow the scale of the Parent View. In this case, the Front Elevation is the Parent View. 




CREATING THE ISOMETRIC VIEW

9.4) a. Click on Model View . Double-click on the "Toggle Lever" under "Open documents:"

b. Select Isometric  in the Orientation box. In the Scale box, click on Use Custom Scale and select 1:1. Click on the drawing sheet to place the isometric view as shown.


 

CREATING THE CENTER MARKS AND CENTERLINES

CREATING THE ANNOTATIONS AND PROJECTION SYMBOL

9.6) a. Click on Note from the Annotation Toolbar. Label the views.


b. To add the projection symbol, click on Note , select Add Symbol    and under Symbol library, select Projection Symbols. Then, select Third Angle Projection and place it under "Angle of Projection".
