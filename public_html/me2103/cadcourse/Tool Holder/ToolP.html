STEP 1: Creating the Body

1.1) Click on New and select  to create a new Part document.

1.2) Select Front plane. (You have to select a plane before you can sketch.)

1.3) Select Rectangle and draw a rectangle.

1.4) Click on Smart Dimension and set the breadth to 40mm and length to 65mm.


1.5) Click on Extruded Boss/Base and set the depth to 100mm. Click on to finish.


STEP 2: Removing Blocks

2.1) Click on the top face. Select Rectangle and sketch a rectangle starting from the position as shown in the diagram.

2.2) Click on Smart Dimension and set the width to 35 mm.


2.3) Click on Extruded Cut and set the Depth to 16mm. Click on to finish.


2.4)Select the top face of the stepped feature. Sketch a Rectangle of the dimensions as shown and set Extruded Cut depth to 36mm.

STEP 3: Creating the Quarter Cut

3.1) Click on Fillet and set the radius of the fillet to 22mm. Select the edge as shown. Click on to finish.


STEP 4: Creating the Additional Features


4.1) To create a hole of diameter 10mm : Select the face highlighted as shown.

a. Click on Circle and sketch a circle.

b. Select Smart Dimension and set diameter to 10mm. Position the circle as shown.


c. Click on Extruded Cut , select Through All. Click on to finish.




4.2) To remove a block from the base: Select the front face of the model.

a. Sketch a Rectangle and set the breadth of the rectangle to 5mm and set length to 14mm.

Position it at a distance of 32mm from the edge as shown.


b. Click on Extruded Cut , select Through All. Click on to finish.




4.3) Removing a block; extruded cut in two directions: Select the plane highlighted as shown.

a. Sketch a Rectangle  and set the breadth to 13mm and length to 50mm.


c. Click on Extruded Cut , select Through All for Direction 1. Check Direction 2 and select Through All for Direction 2. Click on to finish.

 



4.4) To create a hole of diameter 10mm on top: Select the top face of the model.

 

a. Click on Circle and sketch a circle of diameter 10mm. Position the circle as shown.

b. Click on Extruded Cut , select Through All. Click on to finish.

 

4.5) Removing a disc: Select the front face of the model.

a. Click on Circle and sketch a circle of diameter 50mm. Position the circle as shown.

b. Click on Extruded Cut and set depth to 13mm. Click on to finish.


4.6) Extruding a disc: Select the base of the disc.

a. Click on Circle and sketch a concentric circle of diameter 22mm.

b. Click on Extruded Boss/Base and set depth to 5mm. Click on to finish.




4.7) Removing a block: Select the top-most face of the model.

a. Select Rectangle and sketch a rectangle as shown. 



b. Click on Extruded Cut and set depth to 30mm. Click on  to finish.




4.8) Removing a circular portion: Select the front face of the model.

a. Click on Circle and sketch a circle with its center at the corner (shown as a green dot in diagram below).

b. Select Smart Dimension and set the diameter to 54mm.


c. Click on Extruded Cut and set depth to 8mm. Click on to finish.





4.9) Removing a circular portion, center 3mm from edge: Select the plane highlighted as shown.

a. Click on Circle and sketch a circle of diameter 66mm. Position the center of the circle as shown.


b. Click on Extruded Cut and set depth to 32mm. Click on to finish.

4.10) Extruding a disc: Select the plane highlighted as shown.

a. Click on Circle and sketch a concentric circle. Set the diameter to 15mm.


b. Click on Extruded Boss/Base and set depth to 8mm. Click on to finish.



STEP 5: Creating the Chamfer

 

5.1) To create the chamfer: Select the edge as shown. Click on Chamfer . Select Flip direction, and define Distance as 14mm and Angle as 30degrees.


5.2) You can now save this file by clicking File -> Save. Save this model as "Tool Holder".

 


Drawings


Required:

a. 3 orthographic views (Hidden details are not required)

(1) A Front Elevation indicated by the arrow A.

(2) A Plan View.

(3) A Side Elevation indicated by the arrow B.

b. Indicate clearly the angle of projection.

6.1) To create a new drawing document : Click on New and select  .

6.2) To import the tool holder file : Click on Browse and select the Tool Holder part saved in Step 5.2

6.3) To add a front view : Under Orientation, select Right . Under Scale, select Use Custom Scale, set scale as 1 : 1. Position the view as shown. Click on or Press Escape on keyboard to finish.

 

6.4) To add a side elevation and plan view : Click on Projected View . Move the cursor and click on the drawing sheet to add the side elevation and plan view as shown. Click on .


 

6.5) To add the isometric view :

a. Click on Model View or click on Insert -> Drawing View -> Model. Double-click on the "Tool Holder" under "Open documents:".
 

b. Under Orientation, select Isometric , under Display Style, select Hidden lines removed, under Scale, select Use Custom Scale, set scale as 1 : 1. Position the view as shown. Click on or Press Escape on keyboard to finish.

 




6.6) To change Tangent Edge style : Right-click on the Side Elevation View. From the drop-down window, go to Tangent Edge and select Tangent Edges With Font.



6.7) To add the Annotations :

a. Click on Center Mark  from the Annotation toolbar. Select the holes and circular extrusions from the Plan and Front view to add the required Center Marks.

 


Click on Centerline and draw the following center lines as shown.



b. Click on Note  from the Annotation toolbar. Label the views.

 

c. To add the 3rd Angle Projection Symbol, click on Note  and select Add Symbol . In the Symbol dialog box, under symbol library, select Projection Symbols. Then, select Third Angle Projection and place it under "Angle of Projection".

 

6.8) You can now save this file by clicking File -> Save.
