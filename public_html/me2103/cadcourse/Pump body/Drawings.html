Pump Body

Drawings

Required:

1. Add 2 sectional views as indicated by the cutting planes A-A and B-B.

2. Add a plan view.

3. Add 5 dimensions indicated with *.

4. Indicate clearly the angle of projection that you are using.

6.1) To create a new drawing document : Click on New , select to create a new drawing.

6.2) To import the pump body file : Under 'Part/Assembly to Insert', click on Browse and select the Pump Body part saved in Step 5.2.

6.3) To add a Model view : Under 'Orientation', select Right   . Under 'Display Style', select Hidden lines removed. Under  'Scale', select Use Custom Scale and set scale as 1 : 2. Position the first view as shown. Click on or Press Escape on keyboard to finish.

 

 
6.4) To add Projected view : Click on Projected view , position the second view as shown and click on the drawing sheet to place the view. Click on or press Esc on keyboard to finish.


6.5) To create section view B-B: Click on the second view to set it as the active view. Click on Section View . Ensure that the cutting line is vertical. Position the cutting plane such that it passes through the center as shown and click to place it on the view. 

Click on and a Section Scope window will appear. Click on the  beside Drawing View 1. Select Rib and Mirror from the drop down list to exclude hatching. Check Flip direction and ensure that the cutting is in the correct direction.

Click Ok. Place the sectional view as shown.

Change the alphabet to B and click on.

6.6) To create section view A-A : Click on the first view to set it as the active view. Click on Section View . Ensure that the cutting line is vertical. Place the cutting plane such that it cuts through the center of the top circular boss as shown.

Click on Single Offset . Click somewhere above the two eyes of the pumpbody and place the cutting plane as shown. Ensure that the cutting plane cuts through the center of the two eyes. Click and select "Make aligned section view".

Place the sectional view as shown. Change the alphabet to A and click on .

 

6.7) Changing the line style : 

Select Options:

Click on Document Properties -> View Labels -> Section.
Under 'Line Style', select the last line style from the drop down list. Click Ok.





6.8) To create the Plan View : 

Select the first view and click on Projected View . Place the plan view as shown and click ,




6.9) Rearranging the views : Right click on the first view and select Hide. When prompted whether to hide dependent views, click No. Repeat the same steps to hide the second view. Right click the sectional view B-B, go to Alignment and click on Break Alignment. Do the same for sectional view A-A and the plan view. Reaarange the views as shown.

Next, add an Isometric View. Click on Model View and repeat the procedures in step 6.2 and 6.3 except under 'Orientation', select Isometric. Place the Isometric View as shown. Click .

6.10) Editing tangent edges : Right click on the Plan view, go to Tangent Edge and select Tangent Edges with Font. Do the same for the Isometric view. As for the remaining sectional views, select Tangent Edges Removed. Click on the sectional view A-A, select the edge as shown and click Hide/Show Edges.

6.11) Adding center marks and centerlines : Click on Center Mark, under 'Manual Insert Options', select Circular Center Mark. On the plan view, select the outer circle and the 4 small holes on the flange. Click .

 

Click on Center Mark and select the 2 circles on sectional view B-B. Click .

Select sectional view A-A and sketch the centerline as shown.

6.12) Adding cutting planes on the sectional views : Repeat steps 6.5 and 6.6 to create the respective cutting planes. Place the created sectional views outside of the drawing sheet and hide them.

6.13) Add the 5 dimensions indicated with * : Click Smart Dimension and select the outer circle of the flange. Click on the drawing sheet, under Dimension Text, uncheck Add Parenthesis . Click .

 

For the dimension of the small holes on the plan view : Click Note  , under 'Leader', select Bent Leader and type the following text. Click .

Add the 2 dimensions on the sectional view B-B as shown.
