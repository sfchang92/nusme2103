Step 1: Creating the Body

 

1.1) To create a new document : Click on New and select   to create a new Part document.

1.2) To create a rectangular solid : Select Front plane in the Features Manager and click on Sketch    . (You have to select a plane before you can sketch.)

a. Select Rectangle     and sketch a rectangle of any size.

b. Click on Smart Dimension , select the breadth of the rectangle and change the dimension to 62mm ( 6 + 50 + 6 ). Select the length of the rectangle and change the dimension to 114mm ( 76 + 32 + 6 ).

 

c. Click on Extruded Boss/Base , select type Blind and set Depth at 126mm ( 6 + 32 + 25 + 25 + 32 + 6 ). Click on    to finish.

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

1.3) To create the 2 circular bosses : Click on the topmost face of the solid to select it as the sketching plane.

a. Click on Circle    and sketch a circle on the topmost face (highlighted in green).

b. Click on Smart Dimension , click on the circumference of the circle and set the diameter to 37mm (6 + 25 + 6). Next, position the centre of circle to be 38mm (32 + 6) away from the breadth of the rectangular face and 31mm (25 + 6) away from the length of the rectangular face as shown below. Click on     to finish.

c. Click on Extruded Boss/Base , select type Blind and set Depth at 29mm ( 115 - 76 -10 ). If extrusion is into the rectangular solid, click on  to reverse the direction. Click on     to finish.

 

 

d. Click on Bottom View to invert the solid. Select the face as the sketching plane. Sketch a Circle   of diameter 37mm and position the circle such that it is at the centre of the rectangular face, i.e. 31mm (25 + 6) from the length and 63mm (25 + 32 + 6) from the breadth. Click on    to finish.

e. Click on Extruded Boss/Base , select type Blind and set depth at 28mm ( 76 - 32 - 6 - 10 ). Click on   to finish.

f. Click on Isometric view.

 

 

 

 

 

 

 
1.4) To create the curved edges of the base solid : Click on Fillet . Select the two top side edges of the solid and set radius to 16mm (10 + 6). Click on   .

Repeat the procedure to establish the following fillets:-

a. fillet radius 38 mm ( 32 + 6 ) to the bottom outer side edges of the solid.

b. fillet radius 9mm to the remaining outer edges required.

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

1.5) To create a 6mm shell out of the solid : Click on Shell . Set the shell parameter to 6 mm. Select the faces of the upper circular column and lower circular column. Click on   to finish.

   

 

Step 2: Creating the Flanges

 

2.1) To create the top Flange :

a. Select the top face of the upper circular column as the sketching plane. Click on Circle and sketch a circle whose center coincides with the center of the top circular surface of the solid column.

b. Click on Smart Dimension and set the diameter of the circle to 76 mm.

c. Once again, click on Circle and sketch a smaller circle whose center also coincides with the center of the top circular surface of the solid column.

d. Click on Smart Dimension and set diameter of this smaller circle to 25 mm.

e. Click on Extruded Boss/Base , select type Blind and set depth at 10 mm. Click on   to finish.

2.2) To create the bottom Flange: Click on Bottom view and select the bottom circular face. Repeat the procedures in Steps 2.1.

 

2.3)To create a hole on the flange : Click on Top view. Select the top surface of the flange as the sketching plane.

a. Click on Centerline and sketch a vertical centerline that passes through the center of the 25mm hole as shown in the diagram below.

b. Sketch an additional centerline that passes through the center of the hole.

c. Click on Smart Dimension . Select both centerlines and set dimension to 45 deg apart from each other.

d. Click on Circle   and sketch a circle with its center lying along the additional centerline (which is now 45 deg away from the vertical centerline).

e. Click on Smart Dimension and set its diameter to 10 mm. Position the center of this circle to be 28 mm away from the center of the 25mm hole.

  

f. Click on Extruded Cut , select type Blind and set Depth at 10 mm. If extruded cut is not into the flange, click on to reverse the direction. Click on   to finish.

 

2.4) To create 3 additional holes on flange : Since the 4 holes are arranged in a pattern, making use of circular pattern will be more efficient than to create the additional 3 holes individually.

a. Select the desired feature from the Features Manager (In this case, it is the newly created circular hole).

b. Click on circular pattern . Under Parameters, select "Edge <1>": the 76mm circle created in Step 2.1. Set the Total Angle as 360 deg. Set the Number of Instances as 4 and check on Equal Spacing box. Click on    to finish.

       

 

 

2.5) To create holes on the bottom Flange: Click on Bottom view and select the bottom circular face. Repeat the procedures in Steps 2.3 - 2.4. Click on Isometric view.

 

Step 3: Creating the Eyes

 

3.1) To create the outer shape of the eyes : Click on Right and select the face as the sketching plane.

a. Click on Circle  and sketch 2 circles with the same centers as the fillet radius on the bottom two sides.
Click on Smart Dimension and set the diameters of the 2 circles to 50 mm.

b. Click on Circle  and sketch 2 smaller circles as shown.
Click on Smart Dimension and set the diameters of the 2 circles to 12 mm.

c. Under Display/Delete Relations, click on Add Relation , select one of the 12mm circle and one of the 50mm circle. Under Add Relations, select . Click on     to finish.

 

d. Click on Add Relation , select the same 12mm circle and the other 50mm circle. Under Add Relations, select . Click on     to finish.

e. Repeat these procedures for the other 12mm circle to obtain the sketch as shown.

 

 

 

f. Next, click on Trim Entities , select 'Trim to closest'   and trim off the excess lines to obtain the sketch as shown. Click on     to finish.

g. Click on Extruded Boss/Base , select type Blind and set Depth at 3mm. Click on     to finish.

  

 

3.2) To create the holes on the eyes: Select the front face of the eyes as the sketching plane.

a. Click on Circle  and sketch 2 more circles with the same centers as the fillet radius on the bottom two sides.

b. Click on Smart Dimension and set the diameters of the 2 circles to 24mm.

c. Click on Extruded Cut , select type Blind and set depth at 9mm. Click on    to finish.

 

3.3) To create the two holes at the back: Click on Left and select the face as the sketching plane.

a. Click on Circle  and sketch 2 circles with the same centers as the fillet radius on the bottom two sides.

b. Click on Smart Dimension and set the diameters of the 2 circles to 38 mm.

c. Click on Extruded Cut , select type Blind and set Depth at 6mm. Click on    to finish.

 

 

Step 4: Creating the Ribs

 

4.1) To create the rib on the left side:

a. Since the rib lies on the middle plane of the pump body, the line that defines the rib has to be sketched on the middle plane. To create the middle plane, click on Reference Geometry -> Plane.

b. Select the front plane of the pump body as shown in pink as the 'First Reference'. Change the Offset Distance to 28mm and make sure the inserted green plane is inside the pump body (Check the Flip box if necessary). Click on    to finish.

 

       

 

 

 

 

c. Select the plane created in Step 4.1b as the sketching plane. Click on Normal To  . Click on Line and sketch a line that is away from the pump body as shown.

d. Click on Add Relation . Under Selected Entities, select the line and the bottom left curved edge (Note: Select the curved edge, not the curved surface). Under Add Relations, select . Click on   to finish.


e. Click on Add Relation . Under the Selected Entities, select the point at the bottom end of the line and the top edge of the flange. Under Add Relations, click on  . Click on  to finish.

 


f. Click on Add Relation . Under Selected Entities, select the point on the top end of the line and the bottom left curved edge. Under Add Relations, select    . Click on     to finish. 

 

  

 

 

 

  

 

 

 

 

 

g. Select the newly created line and click on Rib . Under Thickness, select Second Side     . Set Rib Thickness to 6mm. Click on    to finish. Select Flip material side if rib direction is not towards the body.

     

 

  

 

 

 

 

 

4.2) To create the rib on the other side : Instead of sketching a new line to create the other rib, use the mirror feature. Under the Mirror feature, a mirror plane to reflect the rib has to be created. This mirror plane lies in the middle of the pump body and is perpendicular to the plane created in Step 4.1b.

a. To create the mirror plane, click on Reference Geometry -> Plane. Select the left or right side plane of the pump body as the 'First Reference'. Change the Offset Distance to 63 mm and make sure the inserted plane is at the middle of the pump body (Check the Reverse Direction box if necessary). Click on     to finish.

b. Click on Mirror . Select the mirror plane created in Step 4.2a as Mirror Face/Plane. Select the Rib created in Step 4.1 as the Features to Mirror. Click on    to finish.

 

 

  

 

 

 

 

 

Step 5: Redefining the Model

5.1) Final touches to the pump body : Click on Fillet . Set fillet radius to 3mm and apply to the edges as shown. Click on    to finish.
 

5.2) You can now save this solid model by clicking File -> Save.
