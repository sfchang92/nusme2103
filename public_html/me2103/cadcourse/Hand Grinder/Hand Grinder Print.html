Step 1: Creating the Housing

1.1) Click on New and select   to create a new part document

1.2) Select the Right plane (you have to select a plane before you can sketch). Click on Sketch . 

 

CREATING THE MAIN BODY

1.3) Using the Line , Circle  of radius 25 mm, Trim  and Smart Dimemsion tool, sketch the profile as shown with its dimensions.

1.4) Click on Extruded Boss/Base and type Depth 15 mm. Click on  . As shown.

1.5) Click on Shell and type Depth 5 mm. Click on  . As shown.

 

CREATING THE SHAFT HOUSING AT THE TOP

1.6) Click on Normal To . Sketch a Circle  of radius 25 mm. 

            

1.7) Click on Extruded Boss/Base and setting to produce bi-direction extrusion of 5 mm and 75 mm, then make sure to check the merge result option. Click on  . As shown.

 

1.8) Click on Normal To . Sketch a Circle  of diameter 26 mm. 

1.9) Click Extruded Cut . Select type Up to Next. Click on to finish. As shown.

CREATING THE BASE
1.10) Click Plan and refer from shaft housing center with dimension of 180 mm. As shown.

1.11) Make use of the created plan, sketch a Rectangle of length 40 mm (20+20) and breadth 14.5 mm as shown.

1.12) Click on Extruded Boss/Base and type Depth 12 mm.

1.13) Make use of the created plan, sketch a Rectangle of length 100 mm and breadth 85 mm with specified positioning. Then, click on Extruded Boss/Base and type Depth 5 mm.

1.14) Click on Fillet . Select the edges of the handle as shown and set fillet radius to 20 mm. Click on  to finish. As shown.

CREATING RIB SUPPORT

1.15) Click Plan at centre of the body.

 

1.16) Make use of the created plane to create Line   between  two edge as shown.

1.17) Click Rib . Select Both Sides and change Distance to 6 mm. If necessary, select Flip material side to ensure direction of rib is inwards. Click on to finish.


CREATING RIB SUPPORT

1.18) Click on Fillet and select the relevent edges as shown. Set fillet radii to 3 mm. Click on to finish.

1.19) You can now save this file by clicking File -> Save. Name this solid model as "Housing".

Step 6: Assembly

6.1) Click on New and select  to create a new assembly.

6.2) Under "Part/Assembly to Insert", click on Browse and select Housing.SLDPRT. Click anywhere on the right window to place the part. This action will set the part "Housing" as the base for the assembly, i.e. it is fixed and cannot be moved.
6.3) To impart the Shaft into the assembly: Click on Insert Component , Browse and locate Shaft.SLDPRT. Click in the right window to place the jaw grip.

6.4) a. Click on Mate . Select the flap surface of the Housing and Shaft as shown. Select Coincident . Click on    when done..

(Note: Click on Rotate View , Zoom In/Out , Zoom to Area and/or Pan to rotate, zoom in/out and/or move the model to aid in the selection of the faces.)

b. Select the cylinder surface of the Housing and Shaft as shown. Select Concentric . Click on    when done.
              


6.5) a. Click on Mate . Select the flap surface of the Long Key and Shaft as shown. Select Coincident . Click on    when done.

b. Select the cylinder surface of the Long Key and Shaft as shown. Select Concentric . Click on    when done.

c. Select the flap surface of the Long Key and Shaft as shown. Select Parellal . Click on    when done.

d. Repeat the steps for Short Key.

 

6.6) a. Click on Mate . Select the flap surface of the Wheel Flange and Shaft as shown. Select Coincident . Click on    when done.

b. Select the cylinder surface of the Wheel Flange and Shaft as shown. Select Concentric . Click on    when done.

c. Select the flap surface of the Long Key and Wheel Flange as shown. Select Angle to make the key slot is align together. Click on    when done.



6.7) a. Click on Mate . Select the cylinder surface of the Ginding Wheel and Shaft as shown. Select Select Concentric . Click on    when done.

b. Select the flap surface of the Ginding Wheel and Wheel Flange as shown. Select Coincident . Click on    when done.

c. Select the flap surface of the Grinding Wheel and Long Key as shown. Select Angle to make the key slot is align together. Click on    when done.

6.8) a. Click on Mate . Select the flap surface of the Wheel Flange and Grinding Wheel as shown. Select Coincident . Click on    when done.

b. Select the cylinder surface of the Wheel Flange and Shaft as shown. Select Concentric . Click on    when done.

c. Select the flap surface of the Long Key and Wheel Flange as shown. Select Angle to make the key slot is align together. Click on    when done.

                   

6.9) a. Click on Mate . Select the flap surface of the Washer and Wheel Flange as shown. Select Coincident . Click on    when done.

b. Select the cylinder surface of the Wheel Flange and Washer as shown. Select Concentric . Click on    when done.

6.10) a. Click on Mate . Select the flap surface of the M10 Nut and Washer as shown. Select Coincident . Click on    when done.

b. Select the cylinder surface of the M10 Nut and Shaft as shown. Select Concentric . Click on    when done.

b. Repeaet for second M10 Nut.


6.11) a. Click on Mate . Select the flap surface of the Housing and Shaft Brushing as shown. Select Coincident . Click on    when done.

b. Select the cylinder surface of the Shaft Brushingt and Shaft as shown. Select Concentric . Click on    when done.

6.12) a. Click on Mate . Select the flap surface of the Crank and Shaft Brushing as shown. Select Coincident . Click on    when done.

b. Select the cylinder surface of the Crank and Shaft as shown. Select Concentric . Click on    when done.

c. Select the flap surface of the Short Key and Wheel Flange as shown. Select Angle to make the key slot is align together. Click on    when done.

6.13) a. Repeat action 6.9 and 6.10 for Washer and M10 Nuts.


6.14) a. Click on Mate . Select the flap surface of the Crank and Handle as shown. Select Coincident . Click on    when done.

b. Select the cylinder surface of the Crank and Shaft as shown. Select Concentric . Click on    when done.

6.15) Repeat action 6.9 and 6.10 for Washer and M5 Nuts.

6.14) You can now save this file by clicking File -> Save. Save this assembly as "Hand Grinder".

Drawings

Required:

a) a fully assembled sectional front elevation on A-A corresponding to the front elevation of the base.

b) a plan view of the assembly

c) an end elevation looking in the direction of arrow B

d) an isometric view

CREATING THE PLAN VIEW

Before you begin, right click on the top of the window and check the Sketch toolbar and the Annotations toolbar. Click and Drag them to the side of the window. This allows us to access the tools easily.

7.1) Click on New and select  to open a new drawing file from the ME2103 template. Click Browse, and open Hand Grinder.SLDASM.

7.2) Under "Orientation", select Front . Set the "Custom scale" to 1:4. This is the scale which we are going to use for the whole drawing. Left click on the desired location of the drawing sheet to place the view. Click  to finish.

   

To set the Tangent Edge style, right-click on the view. From the drop-down menu, go to ‘Tangent Edge’ and select ‘Tangent Edges Removed’.

 

ADDING THE SECTION VIEW A-A

7.3) Click on Section View . Select Vertical cutting line  and left click to place the cutting plane such that it passes through the center.

Click on  and the section view box will appear. Make sure you check the auto hatching box. In the Excluded Components box, select the Rid of Housing, Keys, Nuts and Washers. Click ok.


 

 

**Changing the line style: 

Select Options:

Click on Document Properties -> View Labels -> Section.
Under 'Line Style', select the last line style from the drop down list. Click Ok.

ADDING THE END ELEVATION VIEW

7.4) Click on Projected View . When prompt to select, click on the drawing of Section View A-A. Move the pointer to the left side (to get an end elevation view from direction B and left-click to place the view. Click to finish.). To set the Tangent Edge style, right-click on the section view and end elevation view to set ‘Tangent Edges Removed’.

 
ADDING THE ISOMETRIC VIEW

7.5) Click on Model View . Double-click on the Hand Grinder document. Under "Orientation", select Isometric and set "Custom Scale" to 1:3. Place the isometric view as shown. To set the Tangent Edge style, right-click on the isometric view. From the drop-down menu, go to ‘Tangent Edge’ and select ‘Tangent Edges Removed’.

     

ADDING ANNOTATIONS

7.6) To create the centerlines using Centerline   from the Annotations Toolbar (not this ):

Before creating the centerlines, click on the plan view and under Display Style, select Hidden Lines Visible. Do the same for the end elevation view.

Click on Centerline and under Auto Insert, check Select View.

Select the plan view, end elevation view and sectional view A-A. Click on  to finish.

After creating the centerlines, click on the plan view and under Display Style, select back Hidden Lines Removed. Do the same for the end elevation view.

Centerlines created on the three views:
*Right click and hide unnecessary hidden lines created.



7.7) Select Center Mark  from the Annotations Toolbar. Select the respective features on the drawings to add in the center marks as shown.

ADDING DIMENSIONS

7.8) Use Smart Dimension  to add the 5 dimensions indicated with an *. (Deselect Add Parenthesis  to remove the parenthesis.)

LABEL THE VIEWS AND FILL IN TABLE

7.9) Click on Note   to add/edit the labels of the respective views. Fill in your Name, Matric number, Class, Scale, Date, Tutorial Name and Comments. 

 


LABEL SYMBOL OF PROJECTION ANGLE

7.10) Click on Note   and Add Symbol. Selection the correct projection symbol from symbol library.

 

7.11) You can now save this file by clicking File -> Save.
