Step 4: Creating the Crank

4.1) Click on New and select  to create a new part document

4.2) Select the Right plane (you have to select a plane before you can sketch). Click on Sketch . 
4.3) Using the Line , Circle  of radius 25 mm, Trim  and Smart Dimemsion tool, sketch the profile as shown with its dimensions.

4.4) Click on Extruded Boss/Base and type Depth 3 mm. Click on  . As shown.

  
4.5) Click on Normal To . Sketch a Circle  of diameter 30 mm. 

4.6) Click on Extruded Boss/Base and setting to produce bi-direction extrusion of 12 mm and 8 mm, then make sure to check the merge result option. Click on  . As shown.

4.7) Click on Normal To . Sketch a Circle  of diameter 15 mm. 

4.8) Click on Extruded Boss/Base and setting to produce bi-direction extrusion of 12 mm and 8 mm, then make sure to check the merge result option. Click on  . As shown.

          

4.9) Using the Line , Circle  of radius 25 mm, Trim  and Smart Dimemsion tool, sketch the profile as shown with its dimensions.

4.10) Click Extruded Cut  . Select Type Through all. Click on to finish.

4.11) Click on Normal To . Sketch a Circle  of diameter 15 mm. 

4.12) Click Extruded Cut  . Select Type Through all. Click on to finish.

4.13) Click Plan and refer from body center. As shown.


4.14) Make use of the created plan to create Line   between cylinder with distance of 7 mm from the bottom surface.

4.15) Click Rib . Select Both Sides and change Distance to 3 mm. If necessary, select Flip material side to ensure direction of rib is inwards. Click on to finish..

4.16) Click on Fillet and select the relevent edges as shown. Set fillet radii to 1.5 mm. Click on to finish.

4.17) You can now save this file by clicking File -> Save. Name this solid model as "Crank".
