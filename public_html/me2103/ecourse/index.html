Module background

For years, Designers and Engineers used 2D drawings for technical communication because many components are just too complex to be described. Since drawings are the fundamental of all technical communication, one must learn to accept and understand the drawings, and be able to visualize in 3D and vice-versa.

In manual drawing, certain standards, such as symbols and conventions, have been adopted because they enable the drawing to be produced much faster. However, one must also learn to recognize these standards and mastered them if one is to be successful in any engineering design.

Today, the advancement of IT and 3D CAD software has brought life to the way engineering drawings are presented. Flat drawings on a paper in the past, is now a 3D model that can be rotated on the screen. Something black and dull in the past is today bright and colourful with a wide array of colours. 3D modeling software enables the designers to model/design components and assemblies much quickly and accurately. Modifying of components and assemblies can easily be accomplished. This in turns enabled a product to reach the market in a much shorter time.

However, many design and fabrication industries today have still not fully integrated the 3D CAD into their design office, it is thus important that students are equipped with both manual drawing and CAD modeling techniques. Furthermore, mastering the manual sketching technique enables one to formulate any design idea quickly without resorting to the CAD system.

Module description

The objective of this module is to enable students to learn various standards and techniques of geometrical sketching, engineering drawings and specifications, and learn how to interpret drawings. Students also get to learn and use an advanced commercial 3D CAD software, Solidworks to do 3D solid modeling. Above all, this module aims to expand the students' creative talent and to communicate their ideas in a meaningful manner.

Main topics covered include:

  1. Principles of projections
  2. Isometric and Auxiliary views
  3. Isometric & orthographic sketching
  4. 3D solid modelling
  5. Sectioning and Dimensioning
  6. Machine and Assembly drawings
  7. Drawing standards
  8. Limits Tolerances
  9. GD & T

Learning outcome:

  1. Able to use advance 3D modelling software in solid modelling, assembly and preparing engineering drawings.
  2. Acquire the basic knowledge in engineering drawing principles, tolerances, engineering conventions and representations.

K S Lee obtained his BSc(Hons) from the University of Manchester in 1977, MSc and PhD from UMIST in 1978 and 1982 respectively. He joined the Department of Mechanical Engineering, National University of Singapore in March 1982 as a Lecturer. He was promoted to Senior Lecturer in 1988 and Associate Professor in 1998 respectively. In 1989, he received the Outstanding Young Manufacturing Engineer Award from Mr Frank J Riley Jr, President of the Society of Manufacturing Engineers (USA). He is currently heading the intelligent plastic injection mould design research & development program in the department.
