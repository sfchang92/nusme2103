7.1 Screw threads

Screw thread is a helical groove which is cut on a cylinder or in a cylindrical hole.

7.1.1 Conventional representation of threads

7.1.1.1 External threads

The major diameter is shown by a pair of thick lines, and the minor diameter by a pair of thin lines

7.1.1.2 Internal threads

The minor diameter is drawn in thick lines (the line representing the end of the full thread. The major diameter and thread run-out are shown by thin lines.

7.1.2 Sectional thread assembly

On a sectional thread assembly, the screw is shown as an outside view so its threads cover the threads in the tapping.

Below the screw, the hatching crosses the internal thread.

7.3 Dimensioning of screw thread

7.3.1 Dimension of ISO screw thread

M16 x 1.5 – 6H (internal thread)

M6 x 0.75 – 6g (external thread)

  • M – metric thread
  • 16 & 6 – Nominal diameter in mm
  • 1.5 & 0.75 – pitch in mm
  • 6H (above nominal value)
  • 6g (below nominal value)

Close fit (5H/4h) is used when close accuracy of thread form and pitch is needed

Medium fit (6H/6g) is used for most general engineering purposes.

Free fit (7H/8g) is used in applications requiring quick and easy assembly.

Note – If the thread is left-handed, the abbreviation LH should be used.
