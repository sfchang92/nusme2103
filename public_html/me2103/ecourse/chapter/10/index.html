10.1 Introduction

Welding provides a means of building up an assembly by joining together plates, bars, pipes and other standard sections.

The process is also useful in cases where castings, forgings and pre-machined parts have to be fabricated into a main assembly.

Welded fabrications are usually cheaper than castings for small batch or one-off jobs because the need for expensive patterns does not arise.

10.2 Basic types of weld

10.2.1 Butt weld

The centerline of one workpiece passes through the weld before meeting the centerline of the other workpiece

10.2.2 Fillet weld

The centerline of one workpiece does not pass through the weld before meeting the centerline of the other workpiece
