2.5 Auxiliary projection

An object may have inclined surfaces that are not perpendicular, or right angle, to the principal planes of projection. These surfaces appear to be distorted and their true shapes are not shown in a regular view having orthographic projection. To reveal the true shape of these distorted surfaces, auxiliary views are needed.

An auxiliary view is an orthographic view having a line of sight not perpendicular to the principal planes of projection.

2.5.1 Purpose

  1. Finding the true length of a line
  2. Finding the true shape and size of a plane
  3. Dimensioning the auxiliary surface
