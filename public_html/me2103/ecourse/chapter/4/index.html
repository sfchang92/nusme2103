4.1 Introduction

Interpenetration occurs when two solids penetrate each other to form one solid. In other words, two different objects such as a regular square based prism and a regular triangular based prism join together to make one complete object. The lines created by this join are known as lines of interpenetration. These lines may be either straight or curved, depending on the nature or orientation of the objects involved.



If curved surfaces intersect, or if curved surfaces and plane surfaces intersect, the figure of intersection will be composed of curves.

If two solids that are composed of plane surfaces intersect, the figure of intersection will be composed of straight lines.

The main shapes associated with this topic are:

  • Prisms (both regular and irregular)
  • Cylinders
  • Spheres
  • Cones
  • Pyramids (both regular and irregular)

Note that some shapes may require an auxiliary view for completion of the question.
