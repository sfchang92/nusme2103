1.3 Line works

1.3.1 Line types

  • Thick continuous line

    • - Visible outline & edges

  • Thin continuous line

    • - Dimension & Projection/extension lines


    • - Leader lines


    • - Hatching/Sectioning


    • - Outline of revolved section

  • Thin dashed line

    • - Hidden outline & Edges

  • Thin chain line

    • - Centre line

  • Thick chain line

    • - To indicate a surface that requires additional treatment

  • Thin chain line + Thick short line at ends

    • - Symmetry lines

  • Thick freehand continuous line

    • - Short break lines

  • Thin chain line + Thick ends + Changes direction
    • - Cutting planes

  • Thin double-dashed chain line

    • - Phantom lines

  • Thin continuous line with zigzags

    • - Long break lines

1.3.2 Hidden line drawing

  • Begins with dash, not space
  • No space between intersection
  • No space between intersection at corner
  • Three hidden lines meet at corner
  • Hidden line continued from visible line begins with space
  • Invisible arc starts with a dash
  • Tiny invisible arc may be made solid
  • Hidden arc continued from visible line begins with space
  • Two hidden arcs meet at point of tangency

1.4 Scale

For drawings, the scale indicates the ratio of the size of the drawing of the part or machine to its actual size.

Recommended practice:

  • 1:1 for full size
  • 1:2 for half size, similarly for other reductions
  • 2:1 for double size, similarly for other enlargements

1.4.1 Scale on a drawing

1.4.2 Application of scale

Commonly used in maps for reduction.

For scale 1:7500, 1cm on the map represent 75m on ground.

1.6 Title block

Title block contains vital part and/or assembly information, such as:

  1. Name of company
  2. Drawing number
  3. Date
  4. Title/part name
  5. Scale
  6. Angle of projection
  7. Unit of dimension
  8. Tolerances
  9. Material
  10. Heat treatment
  11. Surface finish
  12. Drawing by
  13. Checked by
  14. Approved by

It is usually situated at the base of the drawing sheet with a border enclosing the drawing views.

Each organisation can have its own unique version of a title block, although there are different standards of title blocks such as:

  1. NUS ME2103
  2. ANSI
  3. DIN
  4. ISO
  5. JIS
