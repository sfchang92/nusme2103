Development process

This website is actually my final year project, and no, I am not studying Computer Science or Computer Engineering. In fact, I am a Mechanical Engineering student. How is this a final year project for ME students, you might think. Same thoughts crossed my mind when I read through all the available projects: audio, electrical, health, programming, material science and what not. I guess this is the way they reaffirm us we are studying the most versatile course ever (heh!).

I always have a certain affinity towards IT stuff, just that I have never ventured into the coding or programming side. Having this as my final year project might be a blessing as this could be the first university project I have strong interest in, not to mention the lack of lab sessions and product fabrication (hello, round belly).

I started out with Adobe Dreamweaver and Notepad++ for lightweight editing. To have a backup for everything, automatically of course, I used my favourite Microsoft OneDrive, and you should too. A few days into it, I have been trying different design, ultimately went for a sidebar menu. Developing a website offline felt wrong somehow. So, I scouted for free web host and domain provider. Initially I chose Googiehost web host, unknowingly brought along 70% server downtime and redirection issue for mobile device. Again, I went for a hunt and settled down with dot.tk domain provider (or freenom) and 000webhost. It has been working well since, and most importantly, no FTP downtime at all.

Two weeks into the project, I experimented around with my software setup. Version control and change tracking were a pain with only Dreamweaver, so I tried my hand with GitHub. At first, it was a total stranger to me, all the pulls, branches, commits, all the more headache I already had. Everything about GitHub seemed to indicate heavy use of command line, which is not my cup of tea, until I stumbled upon GitHub for Desktop. That was the point when I fell in love with GitHub without getting my hands dirty with commands. So, there I have it, the ultimate combo: Dreamweaver, Notepad++, OneDrive, GitHub Desktop and Animatron. Oh did I missed out Animatron?

If you are new to animation, but loved to make a few with the easiest tool and the latest HTML5 technology, Animatron is, dare I say it, the best option you can find in the whole goddamn universe. It is free, fully-online, has an idiot-proof interface and ever so many options. The only caveat is that it eats through your RAM like nobody's business. Use it in Google Chrome, restart it once in a while (everything is saved online in real-time, nothing to worry here), and you will be a happy lad.
