2.2 Familiarizing with SolidWorks Terminology

The common terms used in SolidWorks are:

1. Vertex: Corner where edges meet.
2. Plane: A flat 2D surface
3. Axis: An implied centerline that runs through every cylindrical feature
4. Edge: The boundary of a face. Edges can be straight or curved.
5. Face: The surface or "skin" of a part. Faces can be flat or curved.
6. Origin: A point where the 3 default reference planes intersect. The coordinates of the origin are: (x = 0, y = 0, z = 0)

2.5 Sketching Basic Shapes

Here is a quick introduction to the sketching of the basic shapes and lines and adding dimensions to them. There are 3 basic types of 2D sketch:

2.5.1 Lines

1. While the sketch is active, click  on the ‘Sketch Tools’ toolbar or select Tools > Sketch Entity > Line.  The pointer changes into .

2. Left-click once in the graphics area of the document window to start drawing the line from the start point.  Move the pointer and click another time at the end point of the line to stop drawing.

3. Click on dimension and then on the line, entering the desired dimension in the box that appears.

4. Click on the green check mark to confirm.
Click to view step (25_01.swf)

 

2.5.2 Rectangles

1. While the sketch is active, click  on the ‘Sketch Tools’ toolbar or select Tools > Sketch Entity > Rectangle.  The pointer changes into .

2. Left-click once in the graphics area of the document window to start drawing the rectangle from the first corner.  Move the pointer and click another time at the diagonal corner of the rectangle to stop drawing. Click on the plane that you want to sketch in followed by sketch .

3. Click on dimension and then on one side of the rectangle, enter the desired dimension in the box that appears.

4. Click on the green check mark to dimension one side of the rectangle.  Repeat for the other.

Click to view step (25_02.swf)

 

2.5.3 Circles

1. While the sketch is active, click  on the ‘Sketch Tools’ toolbar or select Tools > Sketch Entity > Circle.  The pointer changes into .

2. Left-click once in the graphics area of the document window to start drawing the circle from the centre.  Move the pointer and click another time at the circumference of the circle to stop drawing.

3. Click on dimension and then on the circumference of the circle, entering the desired diameter in the box that appears.

4. Click on the green check mark.

2.8 Resolving Over-defined Sketches

Sometimes, users may over define their sketches and as a result, would encounter error messages. In these situations, displaying all the relationships can assist users to identify the cause of the error.

1. Click on in the 'Sketch Relations' Toolbar to activate the Sketch Relations PropertyManager, or select Tools ->Relations ->Display/Delete…. All relations of the active sketch can be displayed in the Relations group box.

2. In the Relations group box of the Sketch Relations PropertyManager, a pull-down menu allows users to select/view relations based on the different categories.

3. After selecting the required categories, the relations that are over-defined are highlighted in red. By selecting the highlighted entities, the related entities are simultaneously highlighted in the active sketch in the graphics area.

4. To remove the undesired relation, select the relation and press the Delete button on the keyboard to remove it.

Illustration:
1. A 100 mm square is drawn. However, a parallel relation is added to the two perpendicular lines. This would definitely create an over-defined sketch.

2. When the parallel relation is selected from the Relations group box, the related sketch lines are highlighted.

3. Press the Delete button on the keyboard to remove the parallel relation. The sketch is now no longer over-defined.
