1. Overview

1.1 Introduction

The 3D Solid Modeling on SolidWorks is a Windows native 3D Mechanical design automation software. Being Windows native, the software bears the familiar Microsoft® Windows® graphical user interface. SolidWorks 3D Solid Modeling allows for feature-based part modeling capabilities and it could create designs easily using extrusion, revolution, advanced shelling, feature patterns and holes etc. Besides part modeling, the software allows users to create assemblies where design to the components could easily be changed from within the assembly. SolidWorks also has the capability to generate complete, production-ready engineering drawings effortlessly. The fully associative drawings allow drawing views to update automatically each time the part or assembly design is modified. In this "Introduction to 3D Solid Modeling (SolidWorks)" topic, eDrawings, a new type of electronic file, will also be briefly discussed together with part modeling, assembly modeling and drawing in SolidWorks.

 

1.2 Objectives

The objective of this topic on "Introduction to 3D Solid Modeling (SolidWorks)" is to allow beginners or users who do not have any engineering drawing background to have a head start in 3D Solid Modeling using SolidWorks without assistance from trainers, through e-Learning. This topic covers the basic functionalities that are being used in SolidWorks. It is designed to be broad-based and covers part modeling, assembly modeling, drawing and eDrawings.

 

1.3 Applications

SolidWorks 3D Solid Modeling is used in a wide variety of mechanical designs on the industry level, such as design of medical equipments, automation lines, machining centers, moulds and dies, machinery products, consumer products and electronic products etc. On the educational level, SolidWorks enables students in their lower years of their undergraduate course to create and visualize 3D models easily during their engineering drawing classes. Students could also use SolidWorks to create complex models necessary for their projects in their higher level of their education.

 

1.4 Pre-requisites

This topic on "Introduction to 3D Solid Modeling (SolidWorks)" assumes that users are familiar with Windows environment, such as how to run programs, resize Windows, saving a file in Windows etc. Users should also have a basic understanding of geometry, simple mathematics and mathematical relationships.
